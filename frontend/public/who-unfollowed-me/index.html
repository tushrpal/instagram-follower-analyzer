Why You Can't See Unfollowers in the Instagram App

Instagram deliberately hides unfollow events. There is no notification, no activity log entry, no "recently unfollowed" tab anywhere in the app. The platform shows your current follower count and your current followers list — and that's it. If someone unfollowed you yesterday, your follower count silently dropped by one, and the person disappeared from your list with no record left behind.

This is why so many third-party "who unfollowed me" apps exist. The vast majority of them are unsafe — they ask for your Instagram username and password to scrape your followers list on a schedule, which violates Instagram's Terms of Service and exposes your account to bans, hacks, and data leaks.

The Safe Way: Use Your Own Instagram Data Export

Instagram gives every user the right to download a complete copy of their account data, including the full followers and following lists. This export is the most accurate source of truth available — it comes straight from Instagram's servers, includes every account, and works the same whether your profile is public or private.

Our tracker uses two of these exports — one from today and one from any earlier date — and compares them side by side. Every account that appears in the older export but not in the newer one is, by definition, someone who unfollowed you between those two dates. The tool surfaces them as a "Recently Unfollowed" list, complete with direct profile links so you can review or refollow them.

Step-by-Step: How to See Who Unfollowed You

  1. Open Instagram → Settings → Your activity → Download your information.
  2. Click Request a download, then choose Some of your information.
  3. Under the Connections section, select only Followers and following.
  4. Set the date range to All time and the format to JSON.
  5. Click Submit request. Instagram emails you a download link within 10–30 minutes.
  6. Download the ZIP file and upload it to our free tracker.
  7. Repeat this whole process again a week or month later. Upload the second export, and the tracker shows you exactly who unfollowed you between the two dates.

Apps that scrape Instagram's API have three big problems. First, they only see a snapshot of your followers right now — they cannot reach backward in time to compare against past data, so when you install them they have nothing to compare against and you have to wait days or weeks for the first meaningful result. Second, they're rate-limited by Instagram and frequently miss accounts. Third, they require your password.

Our approach has none of these problems. Your data exports go back as far as your account does, so the very first time you use the tracker you can compare against any earlier export you've saved. Accuracy is perfect because the data comes from Instagram itself. And there is no password involved at any step.

Does It Work for Private Instagram Accounts?

Yes. Your privacy settings have zero effect on what's in your data export. Whether your account is public, private, or business, the followers and following lists are identical in the file Instagram generates for you. This makes our tracker one of the only reliable solutions for private accounts — most scraping-based tools simply do not work for them.
